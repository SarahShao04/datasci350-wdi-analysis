1 Introduction

This report analyses a cross-sectional sample of economic indicators for the year 2022 using data from the World Development Indicators database maintained by the World Bank (World Bank (2022)). The dataset includes key macroeconomic variables that capture income levels, price stability, and economic performance across countries.

The objective of this analysis is to explore cross-country variation in three core indicators: GDP per capita, inflation rate, and GDP growth rate. These variables provide insight into differences in economic development, macroeconomic stability, and post-pandemic recovery patterns.

Using exploratory data analysis and visualisation techniques, this report examines the distribution of these indicators and investigates potential relationships among them. Summary statistics and graphical representations are used to highlight dispersion, skewness, and the presence of outliers across countries.

Understanding the variation in these macroeconomic indicators is important for interpreting global economic heterogeneity and for situating the findings within broader macroeconomic research (Fischer (1993)).
